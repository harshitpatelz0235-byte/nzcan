## The "Red Light" of Death

SolarEdge dominates the premium solar market in New Zealand, known for their highly efficient HD-Wave inverters and individual rooftop DC optimizers. 

However, they are incredibly sensitive machines. Because they handle up to 600 Volts of lethal direct current directly above your living room, their safety parameters are razor-thin. 

If the green production light on your SolarEdge inverter flips to a **solid Red**, and the mySolarEdge app notifies you of a **System Error 18x** (usually followed by two other digits, like 18x86 or 18xB7), the system has gone into emergency lockdown. 

Here is what the specific 18X sub-codes mean, and how New Zealand solar installers diagnose whether the problem is on your roof or inside the white box.

## Error 18x86: The Water Ingress Isolation Fault

By far the most common 18X error in New Zealand (especially during the wet Auckland winters or frosty South Island mornings) is **Error 18x86: Isolation Fault**.

The cables connecting your solar panels together use plastic "MC4" plugs. If the original installer was sloppy, didn't tighten the waterproof gland, or allowed the cables to drag on the abrasive roof tiles, the plastic cracks. 

1.  **The Dew Point:** Overnight, heavy dew or rain covers your roof.
2.  **The Leak:** Water seeps into the cracked MC4 plug. Water conducts electricity. 
3.  **The Short:** The 400V DC electricity escapes the wire, travels through the water, and hits the grounded metal mounting rails.
4.  **The Trip:** The SolarEdge inverter instantly detects this voltage leak (isolation fault), cuts all power to prevent a house fire, and displays 18x86. 

### The Diagnostic Clue
If your system throws **Error 18x86 at 7:30 AM**, stays offline all morning, but magically **turns back on and glows green by 1:00 PM**, you 100% have water ingress. The intense midday sun has physically evaporated the water inside the plug, curing the short circuit temporarily until it rains again. 

**The Fix:** You must hire a certified CEC solar electrician. They will use a specialized "Megger" insulation tester on the roof to find the exact wet connector, cut it off, and crimp a brand new, watertight MC4 plug onto the wire.

## Error 18xB7 or 18xBC: The Hardware Crash

If your app displays **Error 18xB7**, **18xBC**, or **18xBE**, the problem is not on your roof. The problem is inside the inverter itself. 

These specific codes stand for **Hardware Error**. 

Modern SolarEdge HD-Wave inverters (like the SE5000H) use complex high-frequency switching technology. Inside the unit are massive power capacitors. Over time, especially if the inverter is mounted on a sun-baked brick wall facing North without shade, these capacitors can bulge, leak, and fail. 

When a critical internal component dies, the microprocessor senses the hardware failure and permanently locks the system down with an 18xB7 code. 

### Can You Fix a Hardware Error?

No. Unlike a Wi-Fi router, you cannot simply perform a hard reset to fix a blown physical capacitor.

**What you must do:**
1.  Verify the error code on the mySolarEdge app. 
2.  Take a clear photograph of the sticker on the side of the inverter, showing the **Serial Number** and **Part Number**. 
3.  Contact the New Zealand solar company that originally installed your system. 
4.  They will remotely log into the SolarEdge monitoring portal, verify the 18xB7 hardware fault, and submit an automatic RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization) to SolarEdge. 

Because standard SolarEdge inverters come with a massive 12-year manufacturer warranty, SolarEdge will courier a brand-new replacement inverter directly to your installer free of charge, usually within 5 to 10 business days. 

## How to Perform a Master Reset

If you want to absolutely rule out a software glitch before calling the technicians, you can perform a complete AC/DC master reset to drain the system. 

1.  Turn the red rotary **DC Isolator Switch** (located on or directly under the inverter) to the **OFF (0)** position.
2.  Walk to your main house switchboard. 
3.  Turn the **Solar AC Supply Main** circuit breaker to the **OFF** position. 
4.  **Wait exactly 5 minutes.** The LCD screen and all LED lights on the inverter must go completely black. 
5.  Turn the AC Supply breaker back **ON**. 
6.  Turn the Red DC Isolator switch back to **ON (1)**. 

The inverter will begin its 5-minute safety countdown. If the light flashes green and then locks to solid blue or green, the error is cleared. If it immediately turns solid red again, look at your app—the hardware is broken or the roof is wet, and professional intervention is required.
