The **Fisher & Paykel E30 Error** is a "Flood/Leak" detection code. This means the internal sensor has detected water in the structural base tray of your dishwasher, which trips the flood switch to prevent a kitchen flood.

## Step 1: Drain the Base Pan

Before you start any electrical repairs, you must remove the standing water that triggered the sensor.

1.  **Safety First**: Unplug the dishwasher from the wall.
2.  **The Tilt Method**: Carefully pull the unit out slightly (if integrated) and tilt it backwards at a **45-degree angle**. 
3.  **Wait**: Let the water drain out of the base pan onto a towel for about 60 seconds.

## Step 2: Reset the Flood Switch

Once the water is gone, the sensor needs to be "reset" by drying out.

- Use a hairdryer on a low setting to blow air into the bottom vents for 2 minutes.
- Alternatively, leave the unit tilted and unplugged for 24 hours to air dry naturally.

## Step 3: Identify the Source of the Leak

If the E30 error returns after one wash, you have a mechanical leak. Check these common culprits:

- **The Spray Arm**: Ensure it's not split or blocked, causing water to spray against the door seal.
- **Over-sudsing**: If you used too much detergent, the foam can trigger the flood sensor.
- **The Inlet Valve**: Check for drips at the back where the water hose connects.

> [!TIP]
> **Pro Tip**: If you see "F1" error next, that is usually a faulty flood switch itself, not a real leak.
