## 5G is Not Like Wi-Fi

You saw the billboard for One NZ 5G Wireless Broadband promising lightning-fast speeds of over 500Mbps. You signed the contract, received the shiny white Nokia FastMile cylinder in the mail, unpacked it, and plugged it into the wall socket behind your couch, right next to your PlayStation.

You run a speed test and it maxes out at 40Mbps. 

You feel cheated. You assume 5G is a scam and the New Zealand network is terrible. 

In reality, the network is likely fine. The problem is your placement. The 5G millimeter-wave and C-Band radio frequencies used by One NZ in 2026 operate under completely different laws of physics compared to the old 4G networks. 

You cannot aggressively hide a 5G modem behind a TV, in a cupboard, or in the center hallway of your house. It requires meticulous, mathematically optimal placement to function.

## The Physics of a 5G Signal

Older 3G and 4G radio waves are like massive, slow-moving ocean swells. They are low frequency, meaning they can easily crash through a house, bash through internal timber walls, go down the hallway, and wrap around the couch to reach the modem. 

5G (specifically the high-capacity frequencies) is more like a high-powered laser beam. It is incredibly fast and carries 10x more data, but the moment it hits a physical obstacle—like a concrete exterior wall, a brick chimney, or a large leafy tree outside your house—the "laser" shatters.

If you place the One NZ Nokia FastMile modem in the center of your house, the 5G signal simply cannot punch through two layers of internal plasterboard, insulation, and the exterior brickwork. The modem will default back to the slower 4G network.

## The "Window to the World" Rule

The absolute golden rule for the Nokia FastMile 5G modem is **Line of Sight through glass**. 

Glass is one of the few building materials that high-capacity 5G waves can penetrate with minimal data loss (unless your windows have heavy metallic, UV-reflecting tint).

1.  **Identify the Tower:** You must place the modem against an exterior wall or on a window sill that physically faces the local One NZ cell tower. 
2.  **Use the App:** Do not guess where the tower is. Download the One NZ app on your smartphone setting up the modem. It has a built-in compass and Augmented Reality (AR) feature that literally draws an arrow on your phone camera showing you exactly where the tower is in the sky.
3.  **Elevation is King:** Do not put the modem on the floor near the skirting boards. Place it as high up as possible. Top-floor windows, tall bookcases, and kitchen counters are ideal. You want to get it above the height of parked cars and neighborhood fences outside.

## Understanding the Nokia Directional Antenna

Unlike cheap generic modems that have an "omni-directional" antenna inside (which listens in all directions equally but weakly), the Nokia FastMile is a premium piece of engineering.

Inside the plastic cylinder is a **High-Gain Directional Antenna Array**. It is designed to listen very aggressively in one specific direction to lock onto the 5G beam.

Therefore, you cannot just place the modem in the window sideways. You must physically align the internal antenna with the cell tower.

### The Top LED Alignment Ring
Nokia built a brilliant diagnostic tool right into the top of the unit. When you power the modem on and it connects to the One NZ network, look down at the LED lights forming a circle on the top plate.

1.  **The Center Light:** This indicates the connection type. It will pulse and then turn solid.
    *   **Green:** Perfect. You are locked onto the ultra-fast 5G network.
    *   **Blue/Cyan:** You are connected to the slower 4G LTE network (the 5G signal is either blocked or the tower is too far away).
    *   **Red:** No signal at all.

2.  **The Outer Ring:** This is your directional compass. You will notice that the circle of light is not evenly illuminated. One specific edge of the circle will be glowing much brighter than the rest.
    *   The brightest LED shows you *where the strongest signal is currently hitting the modem*.
    *   **The Goal:** Rotate the physical cylinder of the modem slowly with your hands until the brightest LED is facing directly towards the front of the unit (the side with the subtle Nokia logo). When the signal hits the front face perfectly, you are perfectly aligned.

## Common Placement Mistakes That Kill Speed

If you have aligned the ring, the light is green, but your speeds are fluctuating wildly, check your environment for these 5G killers.

### 1. The Colorsteel Roof Trap
Many New Zealand homes have corrugated iron (Colorsteel) roofs. A metal roof acts as a perfect "Faraday Cage," instantly bouncing 5G radio waves back into the atmosphere. Never place the 5G modem in the attic space, and try to avoid placing it under deeply overhanging metal eaves. 

### 2. The Metallic Window Tint Fail
If your house was recently built or renovated to high eco-standards, your aluminum joinery likely features "Low-E" (Low-Emissivity) double glazing. Low-E glass is coated with a microscopic layer of metal oxide. While this keeps the heat in during winter, it violently reflects 5G signals. If you have Low-E glass, putting the modem in the window completely destroys the connection. You will have to test the modem against a standard timber or plasterboard wall instead.

### 3. The Electronics Cluster
The Nokia modem is a highly sensitive radio receiver. Do not place it right next to a cordless phone base station, a microwave oven, or tucked directly behind the massive electromagnetic field of your 65-inch television. Keep it isolated by at least one meter from other heavy electronics.

## What if You Are Stuck on 4G?

If you have walked the Nokia modem around to every single room in your house, tested every window, and the center LED stubbornly remains **Blue** (indicating a 4G connection), you have a geographical problem.

1.  **Tower Distance:** You may simply be outside the viable 5G footprint in 2026. 5G signals degrade quickly. If you are more than 1.5 to 2 kilometers from the tower, your house cannot catch the signal.
2.  **Terrain Blocking:** Even if you are 500 meters from the tower, if there is a massive hill, a dense pine forest, or a large industrial concrete building directly between your house and the tower, the 5G signal will not reach you. 

If you are stuck on blue, call One NZ support. Have them run a diagnostic on the tower's sector load. If they confirm your address is blocked by terrain, ask to be downgraded to a cheaper 4G broadband plan so you aren't paying a premium for a 5G speed tier you physically cannot access.

## Bridging the Wi-Fi Gap

Finally, be aware of the "False Speed Issue." 

You might perfectly align your Nokia modem in the upstairs master bedroom window. The modem itself might be receiving 800Mbps from the One NZ tower. 

However, if you are sitting downstairs in the lounge trying to use your laptop over the Nokia's internal Wi-Fi, the Wi-Fi signal has to pass through the ceiling and floorboards. By the time it reaches your laptop, the speed drops to 60Mbps. 

The 5G connection is not slow; the Wi-Fi distribution inside your house is the bottleneck.

If you have a large multi-story Kiwi house, you should plug a **Mesh Wi-Fi System** (like a Google Nest, TP-Link Deco, or eero) into the back of the Nokia 5G modem. Let the Nokia handle the 5G connection from the street, and let the Mesh nodes bounce the massive gigabit speeds seamlessly through the rest of the house.
