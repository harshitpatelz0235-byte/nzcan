# 2degrees FritzBox: How to Connect Any Cordless Phone via DECT

<div class='instant-fix-box'>
  <p>To connect a cordless phone directly to your 2degrees FritzBox, put your phone handset into "Registration" mode via its menu. Then, press and hold the <strong>DECT</strong> or <strong>Connect</strong> button on the front or top of the FritzBox router for 5 to 10 seconds until the info LED starts flashing. The handset will pair automatically within 30 seconds. The default PIN is 0000.</p>

## The Hidden Superpower of Your Modem

If you have a home landline connection with 2degrees broadband, you probably set your phone up the traditional way. 

You took the large, clunky base station of your Panasonic or Uniden cordless phone. You plugged it into the wall for power. Then, you took the grey telephone cable and ran it from the base station into the green "FON 1" port on the back of the FritzBox.

It works, but it looks terrible. You have a massive knot of cables sitting on your hallway table or kitchen bench.

What nobody at the 2degrees installation team told you is that **you do not need the phone base station.**

The AVM FritzBox router provided by 2degrees (including the 7490, 7560, and modern 7530 variants) is not just a Wi-Fi router. It contains a highly sophisticated, enterprise-grade **DECT Base Station** hidden inside the white plastic shell.

This means you can throw the large phone base station in the cupboard and pair your handsets directly to the router wirelessly. 

## The Benefits of Using the FritzBox DECT Base

Before we walk through the setup, here is why you should spend the 3 minutes required to switch over to this method:

1.  **Massive Cable Reduction:** You only need the tiny, secondary charging cradles for your phones. You can place them anywhere in the house that has a power socket—the bedroom, the garage, the kitchen. They no longer need to be tethered to the router by a phone cord.
2.  **Incredible Wireless Range:** General consumer phone base stations have weak antennas. The FritzBox is designed to broadcast strong radio signals. By using the router as the base, the range of your cordless phone drops will drastically improve, expanding out to your garden or driveway.
3.  **High-Definition Voice:** If you use a compatible handset, the FritzBox supports HD VoIP Voice protocols, making standard phone calls sound as clear as a FaceTime audio or WhatsApp call.
4.  **A Universal Phonebook:** If you pair three different Panasonic phones to the router, they all share a central, digitized phonebook stored inside the FritzBox memory. You add your doctor's number once, and it instantly syncs to every handset in the house.

## Step-by-Step: The Physical Pairing Method

This method is exactly like pairing a Bluetooth speaker to your phone. It requires no technical knowledge and takes less than 60 seconds.

*Note: Ensure your 2degrees home phone service is active. If you pick up a wired phone plugged into the FON 1 port and hear a dial tone, you are ready to proceed.*

### Step 1: Prep the Handset
1.  Take your cordless phone (Panasonic, Uniden, Gigaset, etc.).
2.  Go into the handset's menu screen.
3.  Look for **Settings**, then **Advanced Options**, **Base Setup**, or **Registration**.
4.  Select **Register Handset**.
5.  The phone screen will say something like "Press and hold base button..." or "Registering..."

### Step 2: Prep the FritzBox
1.  Walk over to your 2degrees FritzBox router.
2.  Locate the physical button on the unit.
    *   On the older **FritzBox 7490** (Red/Silver), there is a button labeled **DECT** on the top face.
    *   On the **FritzBox 7560 or 7530** (White), look for a button labeled **DECT** or **Connect** on the front panel.
3.  **Press and hold the button for 5 to 10 seconds.**
4.  Do not let go until the "Info" or "DECT" LED on the router starts flashing. This means the router is actively broadcasting its pairing signal.

### Step 3: The Handshake
1.  Look back at your phone handset.
2.  Within 30 to 60 seconds, the phone should beep and ask for a **Base Station PIN**.
3.  Type **0000** (Four zeros) into the keypad. This is the universal default FritzBox PIN.
4.  Press OK. 
5.  The phone will beep joyfully, the screen will switch to "Handset 1", and the time/date will automatically sync from the router. 

You can now make and receive calls.

## The "Software" Pairing Method (For Advanced Users)

If the physical button on your router is broken, or if you want to configure specific names for specific handsets (e.g., "Kitchen Phone", "Master Bedroom"), you can pair devices through the router's web interface.

1.  Connect your laptop or mobile to the FritzBox Wi-Fi.
2.  Open a web browser and type **192.168.1.254** (or **fritz.box**).
3.  Log in using the FRITZ!Box password printed on the sticker under the router.
4.  On the left-hand menu, click **Telephony**, then select **Telephony Devices**.
5.  Click the button that says **Configure New Device** or **Add Device**.
6.  Select **Telephone (with and without answering machine)** and click Next.
7.  Select **Cordless (DECT) telephone** and click Next.
8.  The software will now tell you to put your physical handset into Registration Mode.
9.  Click the **Connect** button on your computer screen. The router software will broadcast the signal securely for two minutes.
10. Once the phone pairs, the software will ask you to name the handset.

## What if the Screen Says "Registration Failed"?

If your Uniden or Panasonic phone times out or says the registration failed, there are three common culprits.

### 1. The Custom PIN Block
If the PIN `0000` is rejected, someone previously changed the security settings in the router.
*   Log into the FritzBox at **192.168.1.254**.
*   Go to **DECT** -> **Base Station**.
*   Look for the field labeled "PIN". You will see the custom 4-digit number that was set. Type that into your phone handset instead.

### 2. The Legacy Eco-Mode Block
FritzBox routers have an aggressive power-saving feature called "DECT Eco". This shuts down the DECT radio completely when no calls are active. Some older, cheaper cordless phones purchased from The Warehouse or Mitre 10 cannot process the "wake-up" command fast enough during pairing.
*   Log into the FritzBox.
*   Go to **DECT** -> **DECT Eco**.
*   Uncheck "DECT Eco activated."
*   Try the pairing process again. 

### 3. You Have Hit the Maximum Device Limit
The 2degrees FritzBox can simultaneously support a maximum of **Six (6)** cordless handsets. If you are trying to register a seventh phone for the garage, the router will violently reject it.
*   Log into the FritzBox.
*   Go to **Telephony** -> **Telephony Devices**.
*   Look at the list of registered DECT phones. Click the red "X" next to old phones you no longer own to free up a slot for the new one.

## The AVM Fritz!Fon (The Ultimate Upgrade)

If your old Panasonic phones are dying and the buttons are sticking, do not buy another set of standard phones.

Because the router you have is made by AVM, you can supercharge your home phone setup by purchasing an **AVM FRITZ!Fon** (like the C6 or M2 models). While 2degrees does not bundle these, you can buy them at PB Tech or select online retailers in New Zealand.

When you pair an official Fritz!Fon to a FritzBox:

*   **You get a visual answering machine:** You don't have to dial "1" and listen to an automated voice. The voicemails appear visually on the phone screen like an iPhone.
*   **You get Smart Home Control:** If you buy Fritz! smart plugs, you can turn your heater on or off directly from the cordless phone screen in the kitchen.
*   **You get Router Alerts:** If your broadband drops out because the Chorus line failed, the cordless phone will literally ring and display a text message warning you of the network outage.
*   **Internet Radio:** The phone can stream internet radio stations over the Wi-Fi directly to the handset speakerpiece, even if your laptop is turned off.

New Zealanders are incredibly lucky that 2degrees chose to distribute the FritzBox as their default router. By moving your phones to the internal DECT base station, you are finally utilizing the expensive hardware that is already sitting in your house for free.
