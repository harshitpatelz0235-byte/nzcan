# Spark Smart Modem 2 vs. 3: Is It Worth the Upgrade?

<div class='instant-fix-box'>
  <p>The Spark Smart Modem 3 (White) is a massive technological leap over the Smart Modem 2 (Black) solely because it introduces <strong>Wi-Fi 6</strong> infrastructure. If your house suffers from "Micro-Stutters"—where YouTube pauses for 3 seconds despite having full Wi-Fi bars—your Modem 2 is overwhelmed by too many smart devices. Upgrading to the Modem 3 permanently fixes device congestion. However, it will not increase the physical range of your Wi-Fi through brick walls.</p>

## The "Black Box" Bottleneck

If you walk into the lounge of a typical New Zealand home in 2026, there is a very high chance you will see a sleek, upright black box sitting next to the television.

This is the **Spark Smart Modem 2**. Back in 2018, it was a revolution. It proved that ISP-provided hardware didn't have to look terrible, and its internal Wi-Fi antennas were surprisingly strong.

However, the modern Kiwi household has changed drastically since 2018. Back then, a family had two laptops, two phones, and a smart TV. Today, that same family has four laptops, four iPhones, three iPads, a PlayStation 5, an Xbox, three smart speakers, eight Philips Hue Wi-Fi lightbulbs, a Ring doorbell, and a Wi-Fi connected washing machine.

That is 25 devices screaming for data simultaneously.

When you push 25 devices through the older Spark Smart Modem 2, the internal processor panics. The internet connection itself is fast, but the router acts like a single traffic cop trying to manage a 12-lane highway intersection. The result is "micro-buffering" and dropped Zoom calls.

Enter the **Spark Smart Modem 3**. 

## What is Actually Inside the Smart Modem 3?

Spark didn't just paint the box white, curve the edges, and add a price tag. The Smart Modem 3 represents a complete architectural overhaul from Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac) to **Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax)**. 

To understand why this is a big deal, you need to understand how routers actually talk to devices.

### The "Delivery Truck" Upgrade (OFDMA)
The Spark Smart Modem 2 uses an old radio protocol called SU-MIMO. 
Imagine a delivery truck. The Modem 2 fills a truck with Netflix data and drives to the TV. Even if the truck is only half full, it cannot make any other stops. Once the TV gets the data, the truck drives back, fills up with a tiny piece of Spotify data, and drives to your iPhone. It can only talk to *one device at a exact millisecond*. 

Because this happens thousands of times a second, humans rarely notice it. But with 25 devices, the line of trucks waiting at the depot gets massive, causing lag.

The Spark Smart Modem 3 uses **OFDMA**. 
This allows the router to fill a *single* truck with Netflix data for the TV, Spotify data for the iPhone, and a firmware update for the smart lightbulb, and deliver them all simultaneously in a single trip.

This absolutely obliterates internal network congestion.

### Target Wake Time (Saving Phone Batteries)
The Modem 2 forces your smartphone's Wi-Fi chip to stay "awake" constantly, waiting for data packets. This drains your iPhone battery while it sits on the kitchen counter. 

The Modem 3 utilizes a Wi-Fi 6 feature called **Target Wake Time (TWT)**. The router literally tells your iPhone: *"Go to sleep for exactly 4 seconds, I will hold your data safely here, wake up precisely then to collect it."* This dramatically improves device battery life across the entire house.

## Do You Actually Have Wi-Fi 6 Devices?

Before you log into the Spark App and pay $150 for the hardware upgrade, you must look at the devices you currently own.

The Spark Smart Modem 3 is backwards compatible. If you connect an old iPhone 8 to it, it will work perfectly. But it will communicate with the iPhone 8 using the old "Delivery Truck" method. 

**To get the actual benefits of the Modem 3, the devices in your hand must have a Wi-Fi 6 chip inside them.**

*   **Apple:** You must own an iPhone 11 or newer. (iPhone 12, 13, 14, 15 etc.).
*   **Samsung:** You must own a Galaxy S10 or newer.
*   **Gaming:** Only the PlayStation 5 has a Wi-Fi 6 chip. The PS4 does not.
*   **Laptops:** Most laptops released in 2021 or later will have an "AX" Wi-Fi card.

If all your devices are brand new, buying the Modem 3 unlocks their true potential. If your family is still rocking 2018 hardware, upgrading the router is a complete waste of money. 

## The Range Myth: It Won't Reach the Garage

The biggest misconception in the New Zealand telecom industry is that buying a newer router means the Wi-Fi will travel further. 

If your old black Smart Modem 2 cannot reach your sleepout or detached garage, **the new white Smart Modem 3 will not reach it either.** 

In New Zealand, the government (via the RSM) places strict legal limits on the maximum transmission signal strength (+30 dBm EIRP) a Wi-Fi router is allowed to broadcast, to stop neighbors from interfering with each other. 

The Smart Modem 2 was already broadcasting at the absolute legal maximum. The Smart Modem 3 broadcasts at the exact same volume. 

Wi-Fi 6 makes the signal cleaner, faster, and more efficient, but it does not make the radio waves punch through double-brick walls any better. If your primary issue is "Dead Zones" at the back of the house, do not buy the Smart Modem 3. You need to invest in a **Mesh Wi-Fi System** (like Google Nest) with satellite nodes placed organically down your hallway. 

## The Final Verdict for 2026

**You SHOULD upgrade to the Smart Modem 3 if:**
1.  You are paying $115/month for the massive "Fibre Max" 900Mbps plan. The Modem 2's Wi-Fi caps out around 450Mbps. The Modem 3 can push 800Mbps+ wirelessly to a modern smartphone, giving you the speed you actually pay for.
2.  You have over 20 Wi-Fi connected devices (smart plugs, Alexas, cameras) crowding the network.
3.  You are experiencing random ping spikes in gaming while others stream Netflix.

**You SHOULD KEEP the Smart Modem 2 if:**
1.  You are on a basic 50Mbps or 300Mbps Fibre connection. The Modem 2 easily maxes out those speeds.
2.  You live alone or in a small flat with only a few devices.
3.  You rely on physical Ethernet cables. The hardwired ports on the back of the Modem 2 are identical (1 Gigabit) to the ports on the Modem 3. Your gaming PC plugged into the wall will see exactly zero difference between the two routers.

If you decide to upgrade, log into your 'My Spark' portal. Occasionally, if you agree to sign a fresh 12-month broadband contract, Spark will waive the hardware fee and courier the Smart Modem 3 to you for free.
