# Skinny Jump Modem Solid Red: How to Force a Cell Tower Reconnect

<div class='instant-fix-box'>
  <p>A solid red light on a Skinny Jump modem means it has lost connection to the Spark 4G cell tower. Turn off the modem, remove the SIM card from the bottom slot, wipe it with a dry cloth, reinsert it, and move the modem to a window facing the nearest main road.</p>

## Why Your Skinny Jump Disconnected

**Skinny Jump** is a life-saver for thousands of New Zealand households, providing low-cost Wireless Broadband over Spark s 4G and 5G networks. But when that center light turns **Solid Red**, your internet comes to a halt.

1.  **Tower Congestion:** If too many people in your neighborhood are using the same cell tower, the tower may kick your modem to a secondary, weaker tower.
2.  **Signal Blocking:** NZ homes with modern double-glazing can block up to 30 percent of 4G signals, leading to authentication failure.
3.  **Authentication Desync:** Every few weeks, the Spark network refreshes its security keys. If your modem was mid-download during this refresh, the SIM card handshake can fail.

## The SIM Card Re-Seat Trick

A solid red light often indicates the modem s internal software has lost the 'handshake' with the physical SIM card due to moisture or vibration.

1.  **Power Down:** Turn off the modem and unplug it from the wall.
2.  **Eject SIM:** Locate the SIM card slot on the bottom or side. Use your fingernail or a small tool to press and release the card.
3.  **The Dry Cloth Wipe:** Wipe the gold contacts of the SIM card with a dry, lint-free cloth. Do not use water or chemicals.
4.  **Re-register:** Reinsert the card, power the modem back on, and wait 3 minutes. This forces the LTE modem chip to forget the last Tower ID and scan for a fresh, strong signal.

## Optimal Modem Placement in a Kiwi Home

In 2026, signal strength is everything. NZ homes use **Low-E Thermal Glass** which contains a microscopic metallic coating. This coating is amazing for keeping heat in, but it acts as a **Faraday Cage** for cellular signals.

*   **Move to the Window:** Move the modem to the highest window in your house. 
*   **The Main Road Rule:** Ideally, place it in a window that faces the nearest main road, as this is where Spark usually places its micro-cells.
*   **Bypass the TV:** Never place the modem behind a large TV or inside a metal cabinet, as these cause massive signal interference (EMI).

## Checking Your Skinny Data Cap

Sometimes a red light is not a technical fault—it is a billing one. 

1.  **Allocation Check:** A solid red light can trigger when you have used 100 percent of your 15GB or 35GB monthly allocation.
2.  **The App Check:** Open the Skinny app on your phone (using mobile data) to see if you have run out of data.
3.  **Local Top-up:** You can purchase top-ups at **The Warehouse** or **local dairies** if you cannot do it through the app.
