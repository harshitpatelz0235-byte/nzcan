# Chorus Box Alarm Light: What Red Actually Means

<div class='instant-fix-box'>
  <p>A red <strong>ALARM</strong>, <strong>LOS</strong>, or <strong>Optical</strong> light on your white Chorus wall box indicates a physical severing of the fibre optic connection. Check the thin (usually yellow) cable running from the wall plate into the bottom of the box. If it is sharply bent, crushed, or chewed by a pet, the glass inside has shattered. You cannot fix this via software. Call your internet provider immediately and report a <strong>"Chorus Loss of Signal."</strong></p>
</div>

## The "Death of the Laser"

If your entire house suddenly loses internet in 2026, the first thing you should do is look at the white Chorus Optical Network Terminal (ONT) bolted to your wall. 

Usually, the little LEDs are a comforting sea of green. But if the light labeled **Optical**, **LOS** (Loss of Signal), or **ALARM** is glowing solid red, you have a physical hardware emergency.

Fibre internet works by shooting microscopic pulses of laser light down a strand of glass thinner than a human hair, from a Chorus cabinet on your street directly into the white box in your lounge. 

The red ALARM light tells you one very specific, terrifying thing: **The Chorus box is completely blind.** 

It can no longer "see" the laser light from the street. The light is either not being sent, or the glass tunnel delivering the light has been broken.

You can reboot your main Wi-Fi router a hundred times, but it will not fix the internet. You have to locate the physical break in the glass.

## Scenario 1: The Local Street Cut (Free Repair)

If you have definitively not touched the Chorus box in months, the fibre cable has likely been severed outside your property line.

**1. The "Digger" Scenario:**
Fibre optic cables run underground in shallow trenches. It is incredibly common for construction crews, neighbors building a new fence, or the local council fixing a burst water pipe to accidentally drive a spade directly through the Chorus fibre trunk line under the footpath.
When this happens, the light instantly stops flowing, and every house on the street will simultaneously display a red ALARM light on their ONTs.

**2. The "Cabinet Card" Failure:**
At the end of your street sits a large, green metal Chorus cabinet. Inside are thousands of laser-emitting cards. Sometimes, these cards simply fry and die of old age. 

### How to Check for a Street Cut
Switch your mobile phone to 4G/5G data. Log into your ISP’s website (e.g., Spark or 2degrees) and check their **Network Outages** map. If there is a massive red circle over your suburb, Chorus already knows about the severed cable and contractors are literally digging up the street right now to splice it back together.

If the outage is external, the repair is 100% free. Just wait it out.

## Scenario 2: The "Inside the House" Break (Expensive Repair)

If there is no listed network outage in your suburb, the break has happened inside your property boundary, or worse, right inside your lounge.

Look incredibly closely at the thin fibre patch lead (usually colored yellow, white, or black) that runs from the plastic wall plate on your skirting board, up into the bottom of the Chorus ONT.

This is a physical strand of glass. It is protected by Kevlar yarn, but it is highly fragile.

**Common Causes of House Breaks:**
1.  **The Vacuum Cleaner Strike:** The cable was dangling onto the floor, and you forcefully bumped it with the hard plastic head of a vacuum cleaner, crushing the glass inside.
2.  **The "Right Angle" Bend:** You moved a heavy wooden bookshelf to hide the Chorus box, and the shelf pushed the thin fibre cable into a sharp 90-degree corner. Fibre optic light cannot travel around sharp corners; if bent too sharply, the glass simply snaps.
3.  **The Hungry Cat:** Pets love chewing thin, dangling cables. A single, distinct puncture mark from a cat's tooth is enough to shatter the glass core.
4.  **The Gardener:** If your house is older, the fibre might run along the outside weatherboards before entering the wall. If you were aggressively weed-whacking or trimming hedges earlier that day, you likely sliced the external cable.

## How to Get it Fixed Fast (The Script)

If the break is inside your house, you must get a Chorus technician dispatched. 

You cannot call Chorus directly. You must call your retail ISP (Spark, Skinny, Orcon, etc.) because they rent the line from Chorus on your behalf.

Here is the exact script to bypass basic tech support and get a technician booked immediately:

1.  Call your ISP Tech Support line.
2.  **Do not say:** *"My Wi-Fi isn't working."* (They will make you factory-reset your router).
3.  **Say exactly this:** *"I have a Chorus Loss of Signal. The Optical ALARM light on the physical Chorus box on my wall is glowing solid red. I have power-cycled the box, and the red light persists. I need a Chorus technician dispatched to repair the fibre line."*

By using the specific terminology ("Chorus Loss of Signal", "Solid Red Optical Light"), you prove to the agent that the issue is not Wi-Fi-related. They will run a quick automated line test, see that the ping to your ONT fails completely, and immediately book a Chorus contractor (often UFF or Downer) to visit your house the next day.

### The Damage Bill
Be prepared. If the Chorus technician arrives and physically sees that your dog chewed through the cable in the lounge, they will class it as "Customer Damage." 

Chorus will charge your ISP a call-out and repair fee (usually between $150 and $200), and your ISP will add that fee onto your next monthly internet bill.
