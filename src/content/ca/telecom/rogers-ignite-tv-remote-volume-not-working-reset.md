## The "Split-Brain" Remote Control

The Rogers Ignite TV Voice Remote (licensed from the Comcast X1 platform) is one of the most sophisticated pieces of hardware in a Canadian living room. 

Millions of Canadians use it every day to seamlessly shout "Hockey Night in Canada" and have the television instantly switch channels. 

However, because the remote is so seamless, people don't realize it is actually a "Split-Brain" device. It is effectively two completely different remotes crammed into one plastic shell:
*   **The Radio Remote (Bluetooth):** The Voice button, the Guide, and the Channel arrows use Bluetooth radio waves to talk specifically to the Rogers Ignite Set-Top Box. You can hide the box behind a wooden cabinet, and the radio waves will easily pass through the wood.
*   **The Light Remote (Infrared):** The Volume Up, Volume Down, Mute, and TV Power buttons use an invisible Infrared (IR) laser. This laser must have a direct, unobstructed line of sight to the sensor on the front of your Samsung, LG, or Sony television.

When your volume buttons stop working, but you can still change channels, your Rogers remote is perfectly fine. The internal software simply "forgot" the specific Infrared laser code required to talk to your brand of television. 

Here is how to force the remote to re-download the correct codes from the Rogers cloud in 2026.

## Step 1: The 9-8-1 Factory Unpair

Do not waste time guessing codes. The absolute fastest way to fix the volume is to brutally wipe the remote's internal memory and start fresh. 

You must execute the **9-8-1 Unpair Command**. 

### If you have the XR15 Remote:
*(This is the modern remote. The Voice microphone button is a large square right in the center of the directional pad. There is NO "Setup" button at the bottom).*
1.  Turn on your television manually (or use the original TV remote).
2.  Hold the Rogers Ignite remote in your hand.
3.  Locate the diamond-shaped **A** button (triangle) and the **D** button (diamond) near the top. 
4.  Press and hold the **A and D** buttons simultaneously for 3 to 5 seconds.
5.  Watch the LED light at the very top of the remote. It will change from Red to **Solid Green**.
6.  Release the buttons.
7.  Using the number pad, type: **9 - 8 - 1**.
8.  The LED light will flash blue/green. The remote's memory is now completely wiped. 

### If you have the XR11 Remote:
*(This is the older, slightly thicker remote. It has a dedicated "Setup" button at the very bottom left).*
1.  Press and hold the **Setup** button at the bottom of the remote for 5 seconds.
2.  Watch the LED light at the top. It will change from Red to **Solid Green**.
3.  Release the Setup button.
4.  Type: **9 - 8 - 1**.
5.  The LED light will flash green twice. The remote is now factory reset.

## Step 2: The Digital Handshake

Right now, your remote is a blank slate. It does not know how to talk to the Rogers box, and it does not know how to talk to your television. 

You must introduce them.

1.  Stand about 3 feet away from your Rogers Ignite Set-Top Box.
2.  Press and hold the **Rogers Menu Button** and the **Info (i)** button at the exact same time.
3.  Hold them for 5 seconds until the LED light at the top of the remote flashes Green. 
4.  Release both buttons.
5.  Look at your television screen. A massive prompt will appear displaying a **3-Digit Pairing Code**.
6.  Type that 3-digit code into your Rogers remote. 
7.  The remote is now securely paired via Bluetooth to the TV box. 

## Step 3: Triggering the Auto-Audio Setup

Now that the remote can talk to the Rogers box, you can use the Rogers software to download the correct volume codes for your TV.

1.  Immediately after you type the 3-digit pairing code, the Rogers software will throw a new prompt on your television screen: **"Would you like to control your TV's Power and Volume with this remote?"**
2.  Use the arrows to click **Yes**.
3.  The Ignite software is incredibly smart. It will read the HDMI data cord connected to your TV, instantly identify that you own an LG OLED or a Samsung Frame, and pull the correct IR code from the Toronto servers.
4.  The screen will ask you: *"Is the volume working?"*
5.  Press the Volume Up button on the Rogers remote. If the volume changes on the TV, click **It Works!**

### The Soundbar Bypass
If you use a Sonos, Bose, or Samsung Soundbar, the auto-setup might fail. The TV volume works, but the soundbar does not. 

To manually program a soundbar in 2026:
1. Press the **A** button on your Rogers remote to open the Help Menu.
2. Scroll to the right and click **Remote Setup**.
3. Follow the on-screen prompts for "Audio Receiver / Soundbar."
4. You will be asked to type in the brand name of your audio equipment using the on-screen keyboard.
5. The Ignite box will give you 5 distinct Infrared codes to try until your soundbar mutes perfectly.
