# Shaw Arris XB6 Gateway Loud Fan Noise: The Buzzing Fix

<div class='instant-fix-box'>

If the buzzing instantly stops, the fan bearings are shot. Call Shaw (1-888-472-2222) and demand a free replacement.</p>

## The "Mini Fridge" Problem

The Arris XB6 Gateway (the dark grey, rectangular tower with angled edges) was the flagship modem for Shaw and Rogers for several years. It introduced Canadians to Gigabit speeds, but it also introduced a glaring hardware flaw: **Thermal Exhaust.**

Inside the XB6 sits a notoriously inefficient Intel Puma chipset. This processor runs incredibly hot. To prevent the modem from literally melting itself, Arris engineers jammed a mechanical cooling fan deep into the bottom of the casing.

When the modem is new, the fan emits a quiet, acceptable hum. 

However, after three or four years of sitting in a dusty living room, the fan begins to scream. It transitions from a quiet hum to an erratic buzzing, rattling, or high-pitched jet-engine whine that can be heard from across the room. 

Here is exactly how to diagnose whether the fan is simply suffocating, or if the internal hardware has suffered a catastrophic mechanical failure.

## Step 1: Solving the Suffocation

The XB6 draws cool air from the very bottom of the tower and pushes hot air out through the top vents. 

Because the modem is usually placed on the floor, on a carpet, or behind a TV stand, it acts like a giant vacuum cleaner, sucking up pet hair, dead skin cells, and house dust for years. Eventually, the microscopic air intakes on the bottom rim become completely clogged. 

The modem realizes it is overheating, so the motherboard commands the fan to spin at 100% maximum RPM to save itself, creating the jet-engine noise. 

**The De-Dusting Protocol:**
1.  Unplug the black power cord from the back of the XB6. 
2.  Unplug the coaxial cable. 
3.  Take the modem to a clean table. 
4.  Flip the modem upside down. Notice the tiny ventilation slits along the bottom perimeter. 
5.  Take a household vacuum cleaner, attach the narrow crevice tool, and gently vacuum the entire bottom rim of the modem for 60 seconds. 
6.  *(Never use compressed air cans to blow into the modem. This simply forces the dust blockages deeper onto the hot motherboard circuit traces).*
7.  Plug the modem back in. If the fan spins down to a quiet hum after 5 minutes, you have solved the suffocation.

## Step 2: The Gravity Trick (Bearing Failure)

If the modem is perfectly clean, sitting on a hard wooden desk, but the fan is making a violent "rattling" or "buzzing" sound like a playing card stuck in bicycle spokes, the fan bearings have died. 

The spinning blades are physically vibrating against the internal plastic shroud. 

You cannot open the modem to lubricate the fan. But you can use physics to temporarily silence the buzzing so you can sleep tonight. 

1.  While the modem is powered on and buzzing, carefully pick it up. 
2.  Gently lay the modem completely flat on its side (horizontally). 
3.  Listen closely. 
4.  By changing the axis of gravity by 90 degrees, the loose fan blade is no longer sagging against the plastic shroud. In 80% of cases, the rattling will instantly stop. 
5.  You can safely leave the XB6 operating on its side indefinitely, provided you do not block the top exhaust vents. 

## Step 3: The Free Upgrade Swap

If laying the modem on its side does not stop the grinding noise, or if the noise is accompanied by the modem flashing red and dropping your internet connection, the CPU is actively taking thermal damage. The modem is dying. 

You must not ignore a grinding fan. A failed fan leads to hardware overheating, which leads to massive ping spikes, packet loss, and total internet drops. 

**How to get a free replacement in 2026:**
The good news is that Rogers (who now owns Shaw) actively wants to retire the XB6 fleet because it cannot support the latest Wi-Fi 6E mesh protocols. 

1.  Call Technical Support (Shaw: 1-888-472-2222 / Rogers: 1-888-764-3771).
2.  Tell the automated system you have an "Internet Hardware Issue."
3.  When speaking to the human agent, use these exact words: *"My XB6 Gateway cooling fan has suffered a mechanical bearing failure. It is making a loud grinding noise and the modem is extremely hot to the touch."*
4.  Do not say "it is a bit loud." Use the words "mechanical bearing failure" and "extremely hot." 
5.  The agent will immediately flag the modem as a fire hazard/hardware failure and process a free replacement. You will almost certainly be upgraded in the mail to the newer white XB7 or XB8 gateway, which use massive passive heatsinks and are virtually silent.
