## The "3:00 AM" Nightmare

If you subscribed to Telus SmartHome Security in Canada between 2019 and 2026, you almost certainly have a sleek, flat white touchscreen mounted on your wall. This tablet is the **Qolsys IQ Panel 2+** (or the newer IQ Panel 4). 

It is an incredibly capable Z-Wave smart hub. However, it possesses one of the most infamously aggravating features in modern home security: **The Trouble Beep**. 

When the internal backup battery inside the panel begins to fail, the panel emits a piercing, high-pitched double-beep. Because batteries are chemically prone to dropping voltage when ambient temperatures fall, this low voltage threshold is almost universally crossed in the middle of the night, waking up the entire house.

Here is how to brutally silence the alarm, sleep peacefully, and surgically replace the dead battery the next morning.

## Step 1: Muting the "Trouble Beeps"

Your first objective is to simply shut the panel up so you can go back to sleep. You have two options to execute the 7-day mute command.

### Muting via the Panel Touchscreen
If you are standing in front of the beeping panel:
1.  Look at the very top right corner of the screen. You will see a small icon of an **envelope** with a red notification bubble.
2.  Tap the Envelope icon.
3.  Tap **Messages / Alerts / Alarms**. 
4.  You will see a text log reading "System Low Battery." 
5.  At the bottom of the screen, tap the button labeled **Acknowledge All**.
6.  The trouble beep is now suspended for exactly one week. 

### Muting via the Smartphone App
If you are upstairs in bed and do not want to walk to the panel:
1.  Open the **Telus SmartHome Security** app (the one with the green shield).
2.  On the home dashboard, look for a yellow or red warning banner at the top labeled "Issues." 
3.  Tap the Issues banner. 
4.  It will display "Qolsys IQ Panel: Low Battery." 
5.  Directly beneath that text, you will see a highly convenient button labeled **Silence Trouble Beeps**. 
6.  Tap it. The panel downstairs will instantly go silent for the next 7 days.

## Step 2: Replacing the Battery Pouch

Telus expects you to maintain the internal battery yourself. The Qolsys IQ Panel operates exactly like a smartphone; over 4 years of being plugged into the wall 24/7, the internal lithium pouch chemically degrades and stops holding a charge.

**You must buy the correct part.** Do not search for "Telus Battery." You must search Amazon Canada or batteryclerk.ca for a **Qolsys IQ Panel 2 Battery**. 
The exact specifications are: **3.7V Li-Polymer, 3200mAh (Part: QR0041-840)**. It looks like a flat, grey, metallic pouch wrapped in blue tape.

### The Surgical Protocol
Once the battery arrives in the mail from Amazon, follow these exact steps to avoid triggering a police dispatch.

1.  **Disarm the System:** Ensure the alarm is completely disabled.
2.  **Software Power Down:** Go to *Settings > Advanced Settings > Enter your 4-digit Master Code > Power Down*. The screen will go completely black.
3.  **Kill the AC Power:** Find the white power brick plugged into your regular wall outlet that powers the panel. Unplug it.
4.  **Dismount the Panel:** Look at the very bottom edge of the panel. You will see two small plastic tabs. Push those tabs IN with your thumbs, and pull the bottom of the tablet UP and OUT, away from the wall bracket.
5.  **Remove the Dying Battery:** Lay the tablet face down on a soft towel. You will see the fat grey battery pouch sitting in the corner. Carefully unplug the tiny 4-pin white plastic connector from the green circuit board. Then, grab the blue nylon pull-tab and rip the battery out (it is held in place by sticky tape). 
6.  *Warning:* If the battery is swollen and squishy, do not puncture it. It contains flammable lithium gas.
7.  **Install the New Battery:** Remove the wax paper from the double-sided tape on the new battery. Stick it exactly where the old one was. Plug the tiny 4-pin white connector firmly into the green circuit board. 
8.  **Reassemble:** Snap the panel back onto the wall bracket. Top edge first, then click the bottom tabs in. 
9.  **Power On:** Plug the white AC transformer back into the wall outlet. 
10. **The Final Boot:** Press and hold the tiny physical Power Button on the right-side edge of the panel for 3 seconds. The Android OS will boot up, register the brand-new battery voltage, and permanently delete the Low Battery error from the system log.
